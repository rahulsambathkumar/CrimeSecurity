Context


  • Crime a major social and economic issue in the United States. The direct cost of crime was estimated to cost approximately 200 billion USD in 1993 (SOURCE).

  • Becker (1968) a seminal contribution in addressing the issues of criminal behaviour and sanctions. However studies following Becker were unable to estimate the effect of police force on crime.

  • Levitt's aim in this paper is to break the endoginity / simultaneity problem through the use of intrumental variables.

The IV used here in the timing of mayoral and gubernatorial elections


Why?

  • Crime is a political issue- political manipulation card- incumbents have an incentive to increase police force just before elections to project “tough on crime” image

  • Number of sworn officers grows by approx. 2% on average in both gubernatorial and mayoral election but completely flat in non-election years.

Things to remember while using this IV


  • It should affect the size of police force but should not belong to the crime production function

  • Control using other variables to make it unrelated to crime
